# Stage 4 Answer — CA Government Code § 11120 (Bagley-Keene Open Meeting Act)

**Question**: Can the State Bar of California hold public meetings that require attendees to sign in?

**Q&A mode**: standard

---

## Answer

No — if attendance is conditioned on signing in, the State Bar of California generally cannot do that for its public meetings.

- The State Bar of California is expressly treated as a **state body** for purposes of the open-meeting requirements. (Section 11121)
- As a state body, its meetings must be **open and public**, and **all persons** must be permitted to attend unless a specific exception applies. (Section 11123)
- A state body **may not require**, as a condition of attending a meeting, that a person **register their name**, provide other information, complete a questionnaire, or satisfy any other condition precedent to attendance. (Section 11124)

### What is allowed

- The State Bar may **post or circulate** an attendance list/sign-in sheet **only if** it clearly states that participation is **voluntary** and that **everyone may attend whether or not they sign**. (Section 11124)
- For teleconferenced/online meetings where the platform requires information to log in, the attendee must be allowed to use a **pseudonym or other anonymous information**. (Section 11124)

### Limits/uncertainties based on the provided text

- The materials provided do not include any exceptions that might allow mandatory sign-in in particular circumstances; Section 11123 notes exceptions may exist "as otherwise provided in the article," but no specific exception language is included here. (Section 11123)
